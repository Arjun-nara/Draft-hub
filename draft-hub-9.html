Scouting Report
Carter is a prototype edge rusher for the modern NFL — long, explosive, and technically advanced for his age. His first step quickness off the snap consistently puts offensive tackles on their heels, and his array of pass-rush moves (rip, spin, bull rush) shows elite refinement. Against the run, he sets the edge with physicality that belies his frame. The biggest question at the next level is positional fit — true 4-3 DE or 3-4 OLB? Either way, he's a top-five talent with Pro Bowl upside from day one.

Scouting Report
Hunter's tape at Colorado is unlike anything seen at the collegiate level. Playing both ways against Power Five competition, he posted elite production at WR while also holding his own as a starter at CB. His route running is technically sound, featuring sharp breaks, good use of leverage, and excellent body control at the catch point. On defense, his zone instincts and ability to locate the ball are well above average. The NFL will need to decide how to use him — but either answer is a win.
